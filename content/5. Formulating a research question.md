## The Gap in Knowledge
A research question must address a specific "gap" in the current literature. It transitions the researcher from a general topic of interest to a specific area of inquiry.

## The FINER Criteria
A good research question should adhere to the **FINER** framework:
* **F**easible: Can it be done with the available resources, time, and expertise?
* **I**nteresting: Is it intriguing to the researcher and the community?
* **N**ovel: Does it provide new findings or confirm/refute previous findings?
* **E**thical: Can it be conducted without violating ethical standards?
* **R**elevant: Does it matter to scientific knowledge, clinical practice, or policy?

## The Case for Replication
While "Novelty" (in the FINER criteria) suggests finding something new, repeating a study—known as **Replication**—is a valid and vital scientific endeavor.
### Why Repeat a Study?
* **Verification:** Science relies on reproducibility. If a finding cannot be replicated, it may be a false positive or an error.
* **Generalizability:** Repeating a study in a *different population* or *setting* tests if the results hold true universally?
* **Updating:** Repeating an older study to see if results have changed over time due to societal or environmental shifts.
