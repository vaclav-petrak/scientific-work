# Course Tasks Details

> [!NOTE] Course Assessment Criteria
> To pass this course, you must accumulate at least **10 points** by completing the following tasks. These tasks require **substantial** improvements to the visual clarity, organization, and communication of scientific data. All outputs must be in **English**.
>
> | Task | Points |
> | :--- | :--- |
> | Substantial improvement of a **slide** | 1 point |
> | Substantial improvement of a **plot** or **table** | 2 points |
> | Designing a **scientific poster** | 10 points |
>
> You may submit your work continuously throughout the semester.

---

### 1. General Expectations
The primary goal is to demonstrate your ability to independently process and visually communicate scientific information. 
- **Authenticity:** While AI tools and templates can be helpful assistants, they should not be the primary creators of your work. Every word and design choice on a poster should have a specific purpose.
- **Effort:** A high-quality conference poster typically requires 3–5 days of work. For this course, I expect a focused effort of approximately **4–6 hours**. Similar effort should be given to improvements of slides and figure.s
- **Deadlines:** While the absolute final deadline is the end of the exam period in **September**, I strongly recommend submitting your work before the end of the semester to allow for feedback and potential revisions.

### 2. Data Sources & Visualization
- **Preferred Data:** Using your own data from a Bachelor’s or Master’s thesis is ideal, as it allows for the deepest insight.
- **Open Data:** If you do not have your own data, I highly recommend using **Open Data** (e.g., Czech Statistical Office - ČSÚ, or international repositories). This allows you to demonstrate that you can clean, analyze, and visualize raw data yourself.
- **Customization:** Do not simply "copy-paste" existing plots. Re-design them specifically for the format of a poster to ensure maximum clarity and impact.

### 3. Guidelines on AI Usage
AI should be treated as a **collaborator, not a shortcut**. 
- Avoid generic AI summaries of your texts. Summarizing your own work often leads to a loss of nuance.
- If you use AI-generated elements (text or imagery), the final quality must be high enough to justify its use. I value your individual perspective and "academic voice" over generic generated content.
