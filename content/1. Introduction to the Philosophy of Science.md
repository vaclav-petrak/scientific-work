

> [!INFO]  Dictionary definition (example)
>  **Science** is the pursuit of knowledge and understanding of the natural and social world following a systematic methodology based on evidence.

Many see  Science as a Lens - as a specific way of looking at and interrogating the world, distinct from faith or intuition. However:
* **Scientific $\neq$ "Always Right":** Being "scientific" refers to the *method* used, not the absolute truth of the result. Scientific theories can be (and often are) proven wrong later.
- **Unscientific $\neq$ "Always Wrong":** Knowledge obtained without the scientific method can still be correct. For example many natural medicines were discovered effectively through trial, error, and tradition long before systematic clinical trials existed. Science provides the framework to test *why* and *how* they work.

## History: Galileo Galilei & The Birth of Modern Research

### 1. The Pre-Galilean Paradigm
- **Dominant Framework:** Aristotelian logic + Catholic theology
- **Source of Truth:** Authority (Ancient texts, Scripture) and Pure Reason
- **The Goal:** To answer **"Why?"** (What is the Purpose). _Example:_ "Heavy objects fall because they belong at the center of the universe."
- **Demarcation:** Knowledge was defined by its logical consistency with established dogma, not by physical evidence.
#####  Mindset: "Natural Philosophy"
- **Distrust of the Senses:** Philosophers believed human senses were easily fooled (pencil looks bent in glass of water) → Looking at things was considered an unreliable way to find the truth.
- **The "Artificial" Nature of Experiments:**  If you manipulated nature — e.g. by rolling a pencil down a book to slow down fall — you were creating an unnatural situation, distorting the truth.
- **Authority of the Ancients:** Knowledge was found in old books. 



> [!INFO] Who were Aristotle and Galileo Galile?
> **Aristotle (384–322 BC)**  An ancient Greek thinker. His ideas were the main base of Western science for almost 2,000 years. He believed people could find the truth just by thinking deeply and using logic. He cared more about the "purpose" of things than about doing physical experiments to test them
>  
>  **Galileo Galilei (1564–1642)**  An Italian scientist who studied the stars, math, and motion. People often call him the "father of modern science." He changed how we learn about the world. Instead of just trusting ancient books and logic, he believed in looking at nature, measuring things, and using math to prove facts. He proved that many of Aristotle's  ideas were wrong.

### 2. The Galilean Shift: Modern Research
- **Empiricism over Authority:** Truth is found in nature, not books. If observation contradicts Aristotle, Aristotle is wrong.
- **Mathematics as the Language:**
    - _Quote:_ "The universe... is written in the language of mathematics."
    - Move from qualitative descriptions (hot, heavy, wet) to quantitative relationships.
- **The Goal:** To answer **"How?"** (Mechanism/Description). *Example* Galileo abandoned asking _why_ gravity exists and focused on describing _how_ objects move under its influence.
